If you want to study in France, you will be required to get a France Student visa or permit depending on your nationality and condition. The duration of your course also plays a vital role in the France Student Visa Selection.

Different application procedures apply depending on what you will be studying in France, and how long you will need to live in France in order to complete your studies. This can be applicable to students applying for a degree, taking a short course, or enrolling to study French in France.

Types of Student Visas for France:

  • You need to apply for a long-term France student visa if you want to study for longer than six months. This type of France Student Visa (which is also your residence permit) now lasts for the duration of your course in France: three years for a bachelor's, two years for a Master’s, and four years for a Ph.D.. Long-stay visas act as residence permits and are called VLT-TS.
  • Short-stay France visa allows study for up to three months on this visa without the need for a separate residence visa. This is suitable for those enrolling in a language course or other short-term program
  • “Sitting entrance exams” France Visa allows you to take an entrance exam at an institute of higher education. If you pass, then you can apply directly to your local administrative office in France for a one-year, renewable, residence permit.
  • A temporary long-stay France visa allows you to study in France for between three and six months without the need for a residence permit. It is non-renewable.

France Student Visa Application Submission

French universities intake students twice a year. First during the Summer Semester which commences in October and second during the Winter Semester which is available for selected courses and commences in April

Indian students may have to provide biometrics during the visa application process. Your biometrics (it includes fingerprints and pictures) are the proof that countries use for security purposes. You will have to attend a visa interview during the application process.

Depending on your situation, French authorities will allow you to pay this fee online, on location at a visa application center in India, or through a bank transfer. Indian students will not need to get health insurance for the France student visa

After you arrive in France

Within the first 2 months of arrival, visit and register yourself at the Immigration and Integration Office (OFII) and validate your visa.

If your visa reads - “carte de séjour à solliciter” (residence permit to be applied for), apply for a France Residence Permit.

France Student Visa Stay Period & Renewal

  • A short-stay visa will be issued to applicants who wish to attend a training course for less than 3 months.
  • A temporary long-stay visa will be issued to applicants who wish to attend a training course for less than 6 months but more than 3 months.
  • A long-stay student visa France which is equivalent to a France residence permit will be issued to applicants who wish to attend a training course for more than 6 months.
  • The VLS-TS from France is valid for a stay period of up to 3 months or more so that you can stay during your entire degree program.
  • You may need to renew your visa every year if you want to stay in France after your degree is over. You will have to apply for a different visa or get a Fance visa renewal through the immigration office.

After you graduate in France

  • After receiving your Bachelor's degree,= you can stay and work in France only if you have a France work permit. If you wish to pursue your Master's degree, you need to apply for an Autorisation Provisioire de Séjour (APS) which is a temporary residence permit that is valid for 2 years.
  • You can apply for a 2 year France visa extension after receiving your Master’s, Ph.D., or any other post-graduate degree from any university in France.
  • You can apply for a work permit if you find employment with a minimum 1.5 times of the monthly wage in France. After receiving a work permit you can easily get an EU Blue Card.

France Student Visa Processing Time

Make sure that you allow yourself extra time for your France visa application form to process. You should apply for your France Student visa at least 3 months before your university degree program starts.

Immediately after you have been accepted to and enrolled in the University of your Choice, you can already start your application for the French VLS-TS. Once you complete your Visa application process, you can expect the decision in at least 2-3 weeks.

France Student Visa Documents Checklist

    The France student visa requirements for Indian citizens are as follows:
  • Visa application form
  • Original Passport
  • France student visa cost payment receipt
  • Photographs according to France visa photo specifications
  • Original academic education certificates
  • Letter of Acceptance from the French university (proof of enrollment)
  • Proof of sufficient monetary funds
  • Proof of accommodation (if staying in a hostel: letter from the authorities, if staying in a residential complex: copy of lease agreement)
  • Evidence of English Language proficiency – Accepted tests are: IELTS, TOEFL, PTE Academic
  • Evidence of France Language proficiency (if enrolled in a course taught exclusively in French) – Accepted tests are: DELF/DALF, TCF
  • Standardized Tests (where necessary for the program)
  • Two academic references
  • Two references from employers (where applicable)
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Resume/CV
  • Medical insurance
  • Health certificates
  • Draft for application fees, if applicable
  • Other Certificates & Achievements of Extracurricular Activities
