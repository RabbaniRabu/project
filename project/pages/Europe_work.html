What is a Work Visa?

A work visa enables a foreign national to enter a country and work there for a particular period of time. Without having a valid work visa, you are not allowed to take up employment in any foreign country. Different countries have different eligibility criteria and different procedures for issuing a work visa. The nature of a work permit may vary depending on the type of employment; it could either be on a temporary or permanent basis.

More information on the France Work Visa can be found below

A France work visa or a long-stay work visa for France is issued to individuals who wish to work in France for 3 to 12 months. There are many categories of long stay work permits, which you can get after receiving your work visa. Each of the categories of France work permits has requirements and eligibility criteria specified for the type of work that can be carried out with the particular work permit in France.

Depending upon the employment offer, employment contract, and your field of work, you can easily apply for an appropriate France work visa.

Different France Work Visa Types

  1. Short Stay Work Visa:

    2. A Short Stay Work Visa for France is issued to individuals who plan on undertaking short term work or business visits to France. It is a non-renewable visa with 3 months stay period and is valid for 6 months.

  2. Temporary French Work Visa:

    3. If a job requires more than 90 days, you can apply for a “travailleur temporaire”. This temporary France work visa is valid for 3 - 12 months. If you are visiting France to work as a model, modeling assistant, model agency worker, language teacher, and teaching assistant, you can apply for it.

  3. Long-Stay Work Visa France:

    4. If you plan to work in France for more than a year, you will need a long stay France work visa. It is a renewable visa that is valid between 1 - 4 years depending upon your terms of employment. Senior management employees, innovative workers, medical professionals, etc. are applicable for this visa.

  4. Special Case Work Visa:

    5. in certain cases, France issues special work visas, for instance:

Volunteer visa - For applicants who wish to carry out volunteer work in France. It is valid for only 3 types of volunteer work – European Voluntary Service (EVS), Civic volunteering, and association based volunteer work.

A student trainee visa - For students who wish to take up employment that is related to their university courses. This visa is applicable for medical students who wish to take up employment as nurses, doctors, pharmacists, etc.

Types of France Work Permit

After entering France with a valid work visa, you will need a work permit issued in France in order to carry out your job/duties and fulfill the terms of your employment. A list of types of France work permits is given below:

1.Talent Passport Permit

In 2016, in order to attract more foreign nationals to France, the French government introduced the ‘passport talent’ to obtain certain working permits for non-EU/EEA (European Economic Area) citizens. This work permit includes the following categories:
  • Skilled recent graduates
  • Employees on a ‘mission’ with a French work contract
  • Researchers or Scientists
  • Employees of an innovative company
  • Champions of innovative economic project
  • Highly-skilled workers (EU Blue Card Holders)
  • Employees of an innovative company
  • Artists and Performers
  • Company representatives
  • Economic or financial investors
  • A person who has been nationally or internationally renowned for literature, arts, education, sports, science, etc.

If a person has the potential to make substantial contributions to the French economy, be it intellectually, culturally, or even in sports, culture, or scientific and humanitarian manners, they can apply for the Talent Passport Permit which is equivalent to a 4 year France residence visa. depending upon the category, the requirement for the Talent Passport Permit varies.

It can be extended to immediate families such as children and spouses of the visa holder allowing them to receive residence and work permits as well.

2. EU Blue Card

This card is issued to highly skilled workers. This is a 1 – 3 years residence permit. To be eligible for this permit, an applicant must have:

A diploma or a degree attesting to 3 years of higher education or 5 years of professional experience in a particular field. Not just that, the applicant must have at least a year's work contract and earn a monthly salary of a minimum of 1.5 times the French average gross annual salary.

3. Employees on Assignment Permit (Expatriate Employee Permit)

This France work permit is issued to those who have been working for at least 3 months in a company outside France and relocated to one of the employer’s companies based in France and earning 1.8 times the minimum wage. It is a renewable work permit and valid for 3 years.

4. Exceptional Economic Contribution Permit

Foreign nationals are eligible for this work permit in France if they are investing large sums of money or planning to create more than 50 jobs. The Exceptional Economic Contribution Permit is equivalent to a 10 year residence permit in France. The applicant’s spouse and minor children also get the same rights.

5. Scientists/Researchers Permit

Applicants with a master’s degree or higher qualification who needs to carry out research work at the university level are eligible for a temporary scientific activity residence permit

6. Seasonal Workers Permit

A person employed with a seasonal contract work that will last for more than three months is eligible to apply for a seasonal workers permit.

7. Extended Stay Research Scholar Permit

If you wish to work in France with a Student visa, you can apply for an Extended Stay Research Scholar Visa. With this permit, you can work in an educational institute for 964 hours on a 1 year contract between September 1 and August 31.

Eligibility for France Work Visa

Application Process for France Work Visa

In some cases, in order to receive a work visa for France, the employer has to apply and arrange it for the employee. Hence, the first requirement would be to get a job in France.

Also, if you plan to work in France for less than 3 months your employer must obtain a temporary France work permit approved by the Ministry of Labour in France. But, if you plan to work in France for more than 3 months your employer must apply for a long-stay work visa which will also act as a residence permit for you.

  • To apply for a France work visa, the first step is to determine the type of permit you require depending on the nature of your work and employment duration
  • After deciding on the work permit you need to apply for, complete the France work visa application form and arrange for all the documents required for France visa.
  • On successfully filling up the form, you will be required to schedule an appointment to submit your documents and Biometrics at the Visa Application Centre (VAC).
  • After completing your France work visa application and gathering all the documents required, now you are all ready to hand in your application. On the day of your appointment at the VAC, you will need to submit your Biometrics information (fingerprints and digital photograph)
  • Now, that you are done with the France work visa application process, it’s time to wait for the Embassy/Consulate's decision

Processing Time

Typically, the France work visa processing time is around 15 to 20 working days after the submission of all the documents to the French Embassy/Consulate. However, the France visa processing time may vary depending on the type of France work visa you have applied for.

When to apply

You can apply for a France work visa up to 3 months prior to your intended date of travel. This will give you enough time to submit all your documents and make other arrangements properly.

FAQ'S

Q.Is Paris visa free for Indians?

Ans - No. Indians wishing to visit Paris must possess a valid Paris tourist visa or France tourist visa as it is formally known before they plan to travel.

Q.How much does France visa cost for Indians?

Ans - If you are applying for a tourist visa for France from India, you will be required to pay the France visa price of INR 7,199/-.

Q.How long does France work visa take?

Ans - The France work visa can be issued within 15 – 20 days after which you can visit France and get your France work permit. However, your France work permit processing time may vary depending on the type of work permit you have applied for.

Q.Can I work in France with a France business visa?

Ans - No. if you wish to work in France, you must have a valid France work visa and an appropriate France work permit as per your field of work. A business visa for France from India does not permit you to work in France, you can only conduct business or partake in business related activities with it.

Q.Do I need IELTS to work in France?

Ans - France is one of the few European countries where having an IELTS is not required. But, it can act as a bonus and actually work in your favor if you have it. However, having a working knowledge of French is mandatory for many profiles. So check with your employer whether French is a requisite for the job or not.

Q.Is appointment required for France visa?

Ans - Yes. Since the France Schengen visa requires the applicant to submit their biometrics and digital photograph in person at the VAC, the applicants must secure an appointment.

Q.What if France visa is rejected?

Ans - If your France visa gets rejected, you can raise an appeal within 2 months of the rejection. If you don’t wish to raise an appeal, you can simply re-apply for your France visa.

Q.How much Paris tourist visa cost?

Ans - Since Paris is a part of France, the Paris tourist visa price is similar to France tourist visa cost, i.e, INR 7,199/-.
