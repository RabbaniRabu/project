Eligibility Criteria For Dubai Student Visa:

    For Undergrad Students:

  • An IELTS or TOEFL Certificate – exemption from IELTS may be given to students who secure more than 75% marks in English.
  • Proof of 12 years of schooling with minimum 60% of marks – 65% for students of CBSE or ISC board.
  • Some universities also require EmSAT (UAE’s national system of standardized computer based tests) certificate for undergrad admissions.

    For Postgrad Students:

  • Undergrad degree with 60% marks or above.
  • Bachelor’s degree along with IELTS or TOEFL
  • Work experience of 2-4 years if taking admission in MBA programs in Dubai - varies from university to university.

    Gulf Student Visa Requirements:

  • Applicant must have a Letter of Acceptance from the institution they are planning to pursue their higher education from.
  • A Certificate of Continuation is required for students above 18 years of age.
  • Details of visa sponsor – the educational institution, parents or any relative.
  • Approval of relevant General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA).

    Gulf Student Visa Validity:

  • Usually a Gulf student visa for Indians is issued for duration of 1 year only.
  • If a student leaves UAE for a period of 6 months or more their Dubai student residence visa becomes invalid. In such a case, it is the responsibility of the visa sponsor to report the visa holder to the immigration authorities. In order to re-enter UAE they need to cancel their old visa and get a new visa issued.

    Gulf Student Visa Renewal Process:

    Gulf student visa can be renewed for 1 more year after submitting official proof of continuation of study from the institution where the student is currently studying.

    Gulf Student Visa Processing Time:

  • Gulf student visa process can get completed within 2-4 weeks.

    Gulf Student Visa Price:

  • Dubai student visa for Indian fees varies depending upon the university or institution. It also differs in different emirates.
  • There will also be a difference in student visa Dubai cost if your university falls under the UAE Education Free Zone.

    Documents required for Gulf Student Visa Application:

  • A valid passport with atleast 6 months of validity remaining and 2 blank pages (to endorse visa and entry stamps)
  • 4 copies of passport
  • 12 Passport size photographs as per the Gulf visa photo requirements.
  • Letter of acceptance/ Admission letter/ Offer letter from the institute you wish to study in (as a proof of admission)
  • 6 months of updated bank statement as a proof of sufficient funds.
  • Proof of accommodation (tenancy agreement or university’s letter mentioning your stay in university accommodation)
  • Tuition fee receipt of the program enrolled for the current academic year.
  • Visa fees receipt for the current academic year.
  • A certificate of medical fitness.
  • Attested degree certificates for post-graduate students.

    Gulf Student Visa Application Process:

  • As you cannot get a student visa for Gulf from India, you first need to get admission in a university or institution and then acquire a Dubai entry permit to enter the country.
  • After this step, the institution (which must be accredited) will apply for your Dubai student visa at the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs – GDRFA.
  • After your Dubai student visa application is approved, you will receive a Dubai student residence visa along with your Emirates ID card, which is only valid till the visa lasts.
  • Resident expats or relatives can also sponsor your Dubai student visa.

Note:UAE laws do not allow a resident expatriate to continue sponsoring their male children above the age of 18. An exception is given to male students who continue their studies in one of the higher educational institutes in the UAE or abroad. Therefore, male students over the age 18 can remain on their parent’s residence visa sponsorship as long as they provide an evidence of attendance at a higher educational institute for a minimum of one year’s course. Expatriate parents may continue to sponsor their female children for study, regardless of their children’s age.

FAQ'S

Q.Can I get a PR in Dubai after study?

Ans - For outstanding students with a GPA of 3.75 and more, Dubai allows them to avail a 5 year long term Dubai residency visa, with which they can find employment and settle in the country.

Q.Can I travel outside Dubai while I am on a student visa?

Ans - Yes, but you need to come back to Dubai within 6 months. If your trip exceeds more than 6 months than your Dubai student visa gets invalid and you need to cancel it and re-apply for it again.

Q.Do I need to undergo medical test for a Dubai student visa?

Ans - Yes. It is mandatory for applicants to undergo medical tests as part of their student visa for Dubai process.

Q.How much IELTS score is required for a student visa for Dubai?

Ans - You need an overall score of 6 with no band lower than 6, some universities have an exception and accept overall 5.5 as well.

Q.Can I study in Dubai on my Dubai visit visa?

Ans - No. you need to acquire a Dubai student visa in order to pursue your further education in Dubai.

Q.Can I work on student visa in Dubai?

Ans - UAE employers offers off-campus part time job to students only with the permission of Department of Labor. If a student wishes to work part time, off-campus in Dubai, they require permission from their university or institute. The students can also work on campus in libraries, administrative offices, etc. to gain experience. Students can work upto 15 hours/week or 60 hours/month during the sessions and upto 40 hours/week or 160 hours/month during summer breaks.

Q.What are the benefits of studying in Dubai?

Ans - There are many benefits of studying in Dubai: • Cost of living and tuition fees are comparatively lower than other countries like UK, the USA, etc. • Dubai has the world’s top class education system. • It is the safest place to study given its low crime rate/ crime free status. • Since it is one of the fastest growing economies, students studying in Dubai can easily jump start their careers as soon as they complete their studies. • With the influx of students from all over the world, students can easily experience multiculturalism.

Q.When can I take admission in UAE?

Ans - Usually the intake in UAE starts for undergrads in April – May, July – August, December – January and for postgrads in July – August, and December – January.

Q.What are the popular majors to study in Dubai?

Ans - Most popular majors in Dubai currently are – Accounting and Finance, Information Technology, Artificial Intelligence, Computer systems engineering, Marketing, Management, Business Analytics, Mass Media, Fashion and textile designing, Jewelry Designing, Construction, Architecture, etc.

Q.What is a Dubai student visa?

Ans - A Dubai student visa is for students pursuing their higher education in a university or another educational institute located in the UAE.
