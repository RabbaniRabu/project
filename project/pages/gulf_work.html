Dubai Work Visa

When we think of a place that has the most man-made wonders and is a paradise for shopaholics, Dubai is certainly the first thing that crosses our mind. Since this “Land of Superlatives” is pretty close to India, it’s no wonder many Indians apply for a Dubai visa to visit the country for a solo trip or a romantic getaway or a family vacation. It also provides great opportunities for people looking to work in the UAE and improve their quality of life.

With its high standard of living, top class luxuries and no income tax (tax-free status), Dubai has been attracting a lot of expats who are interested in working and living in the UAE. It even came up with the attractive Dubai Golden Visa scheme wherein expats can invest and even own businesses, while living in the UAE.

If you wish to work in UAE, you must first find a job in Dubai through online job portals; job fairs or other reliable recruitment agencies before leaving your country or you can apply for a 14 days Dubai visa, 30 days Dubai visa or a 60 days Dubai visa which are all tourist visa for Dubai from India and go job-hunting in Dubai itself.

Jobs in UAE:

An oil-rich country, the UAE has had to deal with fluctuating oil prices and global oversupply but on the whole, the economy is strong and has seen rapid growth in recent years.

Major industries in the UAE include:

  • Oil and Gas
  • Boat Building and Ship Repair
  • Engineering and Construction
  • Education
  • Information Technology
  • Steel Industry
  • Retail
  • Marketing and Advertising
  • Petroleum and Petrochemicals
  • Hospitality
  • Healthcare

Eligibility for Dubai Work Visa:

  • Must be 18 years old (for applicants over the age of 65 the company has to pay higher Dubai visa cost).
  • Skilled workers must have a Bachelor’s Degree and unskilled workers must have Trade Qualifications.
  • 2-3 years of experience in relevant field.
  • Must have a job offer from the employer and employer should have a valid business license and must not have broken any law.
  • Must meet medical requirements.

Dubai Work Visa Categories:

Based on the education qualifications and skill sets, applicants are divided in 3 categories of Dubai work visa:

  • Category 1: Having a Bachelor’s Degree.
  • Category 2: Having a Post-Secondary Diploma.
  • Category 3: Having a High-School Diploma.

Dubai Work Visa Validity

  • If you will be working in the UAE for less than 30 days, your employer can apply on your behalf for a short term visa. There's also a 60 day Dubai visa (multiple-entry) for those coming for only a few months. In this case, you will have to provide documentation and proof of the business registration before your application can be considered.
  • The general Dubai work visa validity depends upon your employer and your nature of employment. Depending upon these factors, it can be issued for 1, 2 or 3 years.
  • If you’re a student you might be able to work in a relevant position such as an internship, under the terms of your student visa and without needing a new permit.

Documents Required for Work Visa Dubai

The exact documents which you will need to provide vary depending upon the job you applied for. Your employer will first have to make the Dubai visa application on your behalf and will need to prove that the business is properly registered, and that the job you are being employed to do, could not otherwise be filled locally.

Depending on your nationality, the Dubai employment visa application rules may be different.

You can expect to be asked for the following paperwork to support your employer’s application:
  • Valid original passport (minimum 6 months validity)
  • Duly filled application form
  • Applicant’s passport size photograph
  • Application fee – to be paid by the employer
  • Entry Permit issued by the Ministry of Labour
  • Valid health certificate – issued by a government authorised hospital or health centre in UAE
  • Three copies of your employment contract – one for the employer, one for the employee and one for the Ministry of Labour
  • Education Certificates – authorised by the UAE embassy or consulate present in your country and Ministry of Foreign Affairs of your country.
  • Professional qualification certificates – proof of previous employment such as pay slips, experience letter, recommendation letter, etc
  • Sponsoring company’s valid trade license.

    • Note:Additional information and documentation may be required depending on the applicant’s country of origin.

Procedure to obtain Dubai Work Visa for Indians:

  • After you have secured yourself a job in Dubai, your employer will pay and apply for your Dubai employment visa at the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MoHRE) for you which will allow you to stay in Dubai for 30 days (with additional 30 days of grace period).
  • If you have found yourself a job on your Dubai tourist visa then you might need to perform a “Visa Run”, wherein you travel to any neighbouring country like Oman and re-enter Dubai with a your Dubai work visa.
  • As soon as you re-enter Dubai, your employer will visit the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA) and initiate the application process of Dubai Residence Visa and Dubai work permit or Labour Card, an ID card issued by the Ministry of Labour that you must carry with you at all times, incase labour officials spot check you randomly.
  • You must visit an Emirates ID service centre with your valid passport and work visa for Dubai to obtain an Emirates ID for yourself, which will be valid for the same duration of your Dubai work visa.
  • You will be required to undergo a medical examination at any government authorised hospital or health centre for certain job roles as mandated by the authorities for your Dubai residence visa
  • After submitting all the relevant documents at the Department of Naturalisation and Residency Dubai (DNRD) and depending upon your job profile and employment contract your Dubai Residence Visa will be issued with a validity of 1-3 years.
  • After getting the Dubai residence visa and Dubai work permit you can easily begin working in Dubai.
Note:
  • The UAE High Commission also issues Dubai Remote Work Visa that allows visa holders to live in the UAE and work for their current employer who is not based in the UAE. Meaning you can continue working at your current job and move to live in UAE.
  • When you apply for a Dubai residence visa on behalf of any of your family members you will be required to share proof of relationship such as birth and marriage certificates along with other relevant documents.
  • Dubai Residence Visa holders are also eligible to sponsor their parents, spouses, children and even house help under certain conditions.

FAQ'S

Q.How long is the Dubai work visa processing time?

Ans - Depending upon your Dubai work visa application, your work category and documents submitted the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MoHRE) may issue your Dubai work visa within two week to a month.

Q.What are the advantages of working in Dubai?

Ans - The advantages of working in Dubai include – free health insurance, access to housing allowances, flexible working hours, upto 30 days of annual leaves depending upon your company and most importantly, tax-free income – meaning you are not obliged to pay taxes to the government from your salary and you get to keep the full amount you earn.

Q.What is a Dubai work visa?

Ans - A Dubai work visa or Dubai work permit allows foreign nationals to work in the country for a particular period of time.

Q.Can we renew a Dubai work visa?

Ans - Yes, however your employer needs to apply for your Dubai work visa renewal atleast 30 days before its expiration date.

Q.What is the process to renew a Dubai work visa for Indians?

Ans - The Dubai work visa renewal procedure is the same as the process through which you received your Dubai work visa in the first place.

Q.What is the employment visa Dubai price for Indians?

Ans - Your Dubai visa price depends upon your Dubai work visa category, the size of the company and the yearly turnover of the company. By the UAE law, all the expenses incurred in procuring your work visa for Dubai, must be paid by the employer, so you need not worry about it.

Q.Is it possible to get work visa for Dubai from India?

Ans - Yes, if you grab a job in Dubai while you are still in India, your employer can apply and provide for your work visa for Dubai while you are still India.

Q.Is Dubai remote work visa same as Dubai work visa?

Ans - No. With a UAE remote work visa, you can stay in the UAE and work for your current employer remotely. Whereas, with a valid Dubai work visa you can stay in Dubai and work for any employer based in Dubai only.

Q.What are the Dubai work visa medical requirements?

Ans - Dubai work visa has certain medical requirements that need to be fulfilled in order for the visa to be approved. You will be required to undergo a series of blood tests and x ray to determine your overall health. As per the mandate – HIV, Tuberculosis and Leprosy are mandatory for one and all. Apart from these, all women planning to work as babysitters or domestic help are required to undergo a pregnancy test, individuals planning to work as hairdressers or in health clubs, restaurants, food outlets, coffee shops, and other hospitality sectors are required to undergo tests for Hepatitis and Syphilis.

Q.Can I cancel my Dubai employment visa for Indians?

Ans - No, only your employer can file for the cancellation of Dubai work visa.

Q.If I have a Dubai work visa can I bring my family to Dubai?

Ans - Yes, if you meet certain criteria and have sufficient income, you can easily sponsor your family’s Dubai residency visa.
